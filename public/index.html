What is boba?

Boba are chewy tapioca pearls. Tapioca is a starch extracted from the cassava root. Tapioca pearls originated as a cheaper alternative to pearl sago in Southeast Asia, where starch-based jelly-like desserts and drinks originated. Tapioca pearls themselves lack flavor and gel easily, so they are often kept in syrups (sugar, honey, etc.) after cooking to enhance flavor and sweetness.

    What is bubble tea?

    Bubble tea is a tea-based drink often mixed with other ingredients, such as milk and various toppings. I simply use the word "boba" to refer to a drink with tapioca pearls as a topping.

    • Also known as: Boba, pearl [milk] tea, tapioca [tea]
      • ...but never "boba tea." Please don't call it "boba tea."
    • Origins: Taiwan, 1980
    • Main ingredients: Brewed tea, milk (and substitutes), sugar (optional), ice (optional)
    • Toppings (all optional and vary by franchise): Pearl, mini pearl, (egg) pudding, aloe vera, red bean, aiyu jelly, herb jelly, lychee jelly, ice cream, and more!
